# Global Sales Performance Dashboard

##  Project Overview
This project involves a comprehensive analysis of a global retail dataset (9,800+ records). The goal was to transform raw sales data into an interactive visual story that helps businesses understand where their money is coming from and which products are driving growth.

##  Tools Used
- **Power BI Desktop:** For data modeling and dashboard design.
- **Excel:** Initial data source (Superstore Sales dataset).
- **DAX:** Used for creating custom measures and KPIs.

##  Key Features
- **Geographic Analysis:** A world map visual showing sales density by country and state.
- **Category Deep-Dive:** Bar charts comparing performance across Furniture, Office Supplies, and Technology.
- **Profitability Tracking:** Real-time calculation of total orders and customer counts using KPI cards.
- **Interactive Filtering:** Slicers for Segment, Region, and Order Date to allow for granular data exploration.

## Data-Driven Insights
Based on the dashboard visualization, the following trends were identified:

-**Top Product Performance:** Technology products, specifically "Canon imageCLASS" and "Fellowes PB500," are the highest revenue generators, indicating a strong nterprise-level demand.
- **Regional Dominance:** The North American market remains the primary revenue driver, with high transaction density in the Eastern and Western United States.
- **Customer Distribution:** The "Consumer" segment makes up the largest slice of the pie chart (~50%), followed by Corporate and Home Office segments.
-**Order Efficiency:** With an average of ~6.3 orders per customer, the data shows a healthy repeat-purchase rate, particularly in the Office Supplies sub-category.

## Recommendations
To improve profitability and operational efficiency, the following actions are recommended:

1. **Targeted Consumer Marketing:** Since the Consumer segment is the largest, launch a "Product Bundle" campaign for high-value items like Technology and Furniture to increase the Average Order Value (AOV).
2. **Geographic Expansion:** Use the map insights to identify "cold zones" in South America and parts of Canada for potential localized promotions to balance global sales.
3. **Inventory Prioritization:** Stock up on top-performing units (Canon and Cisco products) before peak quarters to avoid "Out of Stock" scenarios that could lead to customer churn.
4. **Logistics Optimization:** Analyze the shipping routes for the North American region to reduce delivery times, as this is where the highest volume of orders (5K) is concentrated.
